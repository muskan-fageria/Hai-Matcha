01 / TEMPERATURE

Heat the Water

Heat up some filtered water in an electric kettle to a temperature of 140-175°F (60-80℃).

02 / THE BOWL

Sift the Powder

Sift 2g of matcha powder into the bottom of the bowl to remove the clumps.

03 / THE PASTE

Smooth the Paste

Add in a small splash of water to the powder and smooth it into a paste in order to remove the clumps.

04 / THE POUR

Combine the Tea

Pour in 100ml of water and gently scrape off the sides and bottom of the tea bowl to make sure it is all combined.

05 / THE WHISK

The Zigzag Motion

Use rapid zigzag motions to whisk up the tea using your bamboo whisk until a thick foam appears on top.

OUR PROCESS

How do we get the best matcha for you?

Scroll to follow the journey of our leaves from the shade-protected fields of Japan to your cup.

01 / THE SOURCE

Shizuoka Fields

Hai Matcha comes directly from the organic tea fields of Shizuoka, Japan.

02 / THE SHADE

Net Protected

Tea plants are covered with nets to protect them from sunlight, so your matcha never tastes bitter.

03 / THE SELECT

First-Flush Sprouts

Only the top 3 sprouts of the tea plants are selected, ensuring you have the smoothest matcha!

04 / THE HARVEST

Steamed & Dried

Perfectly picked leaves are steamed and dried, and then the stems are removed to secure absolute purity.

05 / THE GRIND

Hand-Milled Tencha

The "tencha" leaves are then ground into the finest powder by a traditional stone hand mill.

06 / THE PACKING

Sent with Love

This matcha is then packed with love and sent to you, hope you enjoy it!

OUR ORIGIN

Sourced from Shizuoka, the Heart of Japanese Tea

Shizuoka lies situated at the foot of Mount Fuji, where mineral-rich soil yields the highest-grade tencha leaves. In early spring, our fields are carefully covered with black shade nets to protect them from direct sunlight, preventing any bitterness. Only the youngest, first-flush leaves are selected, steamed and dried, and then de-stemmed before being ground into the finest powder to preserve absolute freshness.

We work directly with organic family farms in Shizuoka, ensuring that every tin of Hai Matcha respects both traditional heritage and ecological harmony.
